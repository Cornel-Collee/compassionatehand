Our Programs

Sustainable Widows Program

Our program aims to provide sustainable support for widows in rural communities by ensuring they have access to financial resources, healthcare, and social networks. These services are designed to help them lead independent and dignified lives. James 1:27 (NIV): "Religion that God our Father accepts as pure and faultless is this: to look after orphans and widows in their distress."


Youth Life Skills Development

Through targeted training in IT, entrepreneurship, and soft skills, we empower youth to take charge of their futures. This program helps young people develop essential skills needed for personal and professional growth. Jeremiah 29:11 (NIV): "For I know the plans I have for you, plans to prosper you and not to harm you, plans to give you hope and a future."


Orphan Support Program

Our orphan support program is designed to assist orphaned children with their educational needs, providing them with scholarships, school supplies, and mentorship to ensure they have the opportunity to succeed in life. Matthew 19:14 (NIV): Jesus said, "Let the little children come to me, and do not hinder them, for the kingdom of heaven belongs to such as these."


Implementation Strategy

To kick start and sustain these initiatives, below, we outline the specific needs for each program and our implementation strategy.

Sustainable Widows Program with Income-Generating Activities

Objectives:

  • Generate income to support elderly widows without requiring their direct involvement.
  • Reduce reliance on external donations through sustainable income-generating activities.
  • Foster community involvement and responsibility.

Activities:

  • A. Needs Assessment:
    • Conduct surveys and home visits to understand the specific needs of elderly widows.
    • Identify the most vulnerable widows for immediate support.
  • B. Charity Support:
    • Provide monthly financial assistance for basic needs.
    • Distribute care packages with essential items.
  • C. Healthcare Services:
    • Partner with local healthcare providers for free/subsidized medical check-ups, medications, and treatments.
    • Organize health camps and mobile clinics for accessibility.
  • Income-Generating Activities:
    • Community Farm: Produce vegetables and fruits for sale. Engage community members in farming activities.
    • Poultry: Manage poultry and operations. Sell eggs, chicken.
    • Tailoring Workshop: Produce school uniforms and custom garments for sale. Offer repair and alteration services.

Resources Needed:

  • Financial resources for monthly stipends and care packages
  • Partnerships with healthcare providers
  • Materials for income-generating activities

Monitoring and Evaluation:

  • Track the number of widows receiving financial assistance and care packages.
  • Monitor health and well-being indicators through regular check-ups and surveys.
  • Gather feedback from widows to assess the impact of the support provided.

Youth Life Skills Development Program

Objectives:

  • Equip youths with IT and entrepreneurship skills.
  • Foster self-reliance and job readiness.
  • Encourage innovation and creativity.

Activities:

  • A. Information Technology Training and Innovation Hubs:
    • Set up computer labs with internet access.
    • Offer courses in basic computer skills, coding, web development, AI, and digital marketing.
    • Create spaces for youths to work on projects, collaborate, and access resources.
  • B. Entrepreneurship Workshops:
    • Provide training in tailoring, garment making, and other vocational skills.
    • Partner with local entrepreneurs for hands-on mentorship.
  • C. Job Readiness Support:
    • Host job readiness workshops, covering CV writing, interview preparation, and professional skills.
    • Organize job fairs to connect youths with potential employers.

Resources Needed:

  • Funding for computers, software, and internet access
  • Partnerships with local businesses and mentors
  • Materials for workshops and practical sessions

Monitoring and Evaluation:

  • Track the number of youths trained and the number of youths who find employment or start businesses.
